About Card Forest.

A credit card allows you to borrow money from a bank to make purchases, whether you’re buying a burger or a round-trip ticket to France.

As long as you pay back the money you borrow within the “grace period” of 25-30 days, you don’t have to pay extra. If you don’t pay it back in that time period, you’ll have to pay interest — a percentage of the money you owe the bank — on top of what you borrowed.
